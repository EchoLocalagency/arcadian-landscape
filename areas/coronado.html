Landscaping in Coronado, California

Arcadian Landscape designs and builds outdoor spaces across Coronado, from Coronado Cays HOA-approved front yards to backyards a block off Orange Avenue. We pull C-27 permitted work, handle salt-air specifications, and run drip irrigation tuned for sandy island soil.

Coronado is its own thing. Salt spray pushes inland from North Beach and Centennial Park almost every afternoon. The soil is sandy, alkaline, and drains fast. Most lawns here struggle by mid-summer because the irrigation was set up for a different city.

What we install in Coronado

Our crews work the whole island, from Glorietta Bay condos near the bridge to single-family lots between 7th and Pomona. We handle landscape design, hardscape, paver driveways, irrigation rebuilds, lighting, and artificial grass for clients who can't keep real lawn alive in the wind.

A typical Coronado front yard rebuild runs $18,000 to $45,000. Backyards with a paver patio, BBQ island, and lighting climb past $80,000.

Plant choices that survive the salt

We plant materials that hold up to brackish irrigation and onshore wind. Agave attenuata, Westringia, Carex pansa, and Phormium handle Coronado conditions. We stay away from boxwood and most Japanese maples here. They burn within a season.

Coronado Cays HOA approvals

Cays projects need plan submittals to the master association before any soil moves. We've pulled approvals on Antigua Court, Green Turtle Road, and a few of the Trinidad lots. Plan reviews take three to five weeks. Build that into the schedule.

Common Coronado yard problems

  • Irrigation valves rotted out from sea-air corrosion. We replace brass with schedule-80 PVC manifolds.
  • Sod yards browning where spray heads can't keep up with sandy drainage.
  • Pavers heaving where bedding sand was laid under 1.5 inches.
  • Low-voltage transformers failing within two years from salt intrusion.
