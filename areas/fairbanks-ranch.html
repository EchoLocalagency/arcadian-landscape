Landscape Design in Fairbanks Ranch

Fairbanks Ranch homes sit on 1 to 5 acre lots inside a gated Covenant community east of Rancho Santa Fe. We design and build landscapes that clear Architectural Review, handle the clay-heavy soil off Via de la Valle, and match the Mediterranean and ranch-style architecture most of the neighborhood carries.

Last updated: April 2026

We have crews in Fairbanks Ranch most weeks. The community sits just east of Rancho Santa Fe proper, bordered by El Camino Real, Via de la Valle, and the San Dieguito River valley.

What the Covenant Actually Requires

Every exterior change needs Architectural Review Committee approval. That includes hardscape, pool surrounds, lighting fixtures over 80 watts, front-yard plant swaps, and any hedge trained over 6 feet.

We have pulled 40-plus approvals out of the Fairbanks Ranch ARC. Submittal turnaround averages 4 to 6 weeks. Budget that into the schedule before the concrete truck gets booked.

What Works on These Lots

The soil is clay-heavy with decomposed granite pockets. Drainage matters more than plant selection on most of these sites. On a 2-acre parcel off Avenida Maravillas, we ran 340 linear feet of French drain before the client lost their olive grove to winter saturation.

Plant palette we lean into: olive, manzanita, rosemary, lavender, coast live oak, and Mediterranean fan palm. Plants that handle the inland heat spike plus the marine moisture that rolls in off the San Dieguito at dawn.

  • Outdoor kitchens and BBQ islands

Cost Ranges Here

Full-yard design and build on a 1-acre lot runs $180,000 to $450,000 depending on pool work, hardscape volume, and plant maturity. Renovations of an existing landscape usually land between $65,000 and $180,000.

We do not quote sight unseen. Every project starts with a walk-through with Evan before numbers hit paper.

How We Handle ARC Submittals

We prepare the full packet: site plan, planting plan, hardscape elevations, lighting fixture cut sheets, material samples, and drainage calculations. Most clients hand us the approval task and we run it through the Fairbanks Ranch HOA office directly.

If you are vetting contractors, our blog post on how to vet a San Diego landscape contractor is worth a read before you sign anything. We also serve Rancho Santa Fe, Del Mar, and Solana Beach.

If we handle your Fairbanks Ranch project, we would appreciate a Google review that mentions the neighborhood and the scope. It helps other homeowners inside the Covenant find us.
