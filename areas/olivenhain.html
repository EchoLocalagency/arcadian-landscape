Olivenhain lots run bigger than the rest of Encinitas. Half-acre and full-acre parcels are the norm. That changes everything about how a yard gets built.

Wells, septic systems, and Olivenhain Municipal Water District meters sit in different spots than tract-home neighborhoods. Driveway runs are longer. Soil swings from decomposed granite on the ridges to heavy clay near the lower drainages off Lone Jack Road.

Olivenhain Irrigation

OMWD water rates are not friendly to oversized lawns. Smart controllers with rain sensors and weather data drop water bills 25 to 40 percent on most properties we retrofit. We pressure-test every existing irrigation main before we touch it. A 60 PSI static reading at the meter does not mean much if there's a slab leak somewhere downstream of it.

For acreage, we usually run two or three independent valves off the mainline so a single broken head does not strand water across the whole property.

Olivenhain Hardscape and Retaining Walls

The CSLB cap on unlicensed work is $500. Most Olivenhain hardscape projects clear that in materials alone, which is why a real contractor's license matters here. We pull permits when retaining walls cross four feet, when grading triggers Encinitas drainage review, and when foundation-adjacent work could affect existing structures.

Our crew runs Class II base in two-inch lifts with a plate compactor on every patio install. Joint stabilizer rated for marine air. Pitch always away from the house, toward yard or a french drain.
