Landscape Design and Construction in Rancho Bernardo

Rancho Bernardo yards come with their own list of problems. Clay soil that holds water in January and turns to brick in August. Slopes off Bernardo Heights and Westwood that shed runoff toward the house instead of away from it. And an HOA that wants to see your plant palette before a shovel touches the ground.

We work these yards the way they need to be worked. Evan Weisman walks the property first, reads the grade and the drainage, then designs from there.

What we run into off I-15 and Rancho Bernardo Road

The east-facing lots near Lake Hodges bake in the afternoon. We plant for that. Low-water shrubs, decomposed granite paths, and drip irrigation zoned so the slope and the flat get watered on separate schedules.

This is fire country. The 2007 burn came through here, and defensible-space setbacks still shape what we put within 30 feet of a structure. We keep that zone lean and irrigated, and we say no to anything resinous close to the house.

Clay is the other constant. On a recent Bernardo Heights build we amended the top 12 inches and cut a French drain along the uphill fence line before laying any pavers, because water was pooling against a retaining wall that never had a drainage plan.

HOA-friendly plans

Most of Rancho Bernardo sits under an association. We build the plan package the way the architectural committee wants to see it, scaled drawings and a plant list, so approval does not stall your project for a month.

What a Rancho Bernardo project runs

A front-yard renovation with new planting, drip, and a DG path usually lands in the mid four figures. A full backyard build with pavers, a retaining wall, and lighting runs into the tens of thousands, depending on grade and square footage. We give you a real number after the site walk, not a guess over the phone.

We handle landscape design, paver installation, hardscape, irrigation, and lighting across Rancho Bernardo and the rest of North County San Diego. See finished work in our gallery.
